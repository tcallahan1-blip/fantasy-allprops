# Fantasy AllProps — Pick Types Reference
Season 1: August 2026 – July 2027

---

## Pick Type Codes

The event catalogue uses three codes to classify how a pick is submitted:

| Code | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| **P** | Pre-Season Pick | Submitted before the season opens; long-horizon outcome |
| **M** | Monthly Pick | Submitted at the start of the event's month; recent-form edge |
| **X** | Pop Prop | Short window; opens close to the event itself |

---

## Pre-Season Picks (P)

Pre-season picks are the strategic backbone of the league. Players lock their calls before August 1 and live with them for the entire season.

**Good candidates:**
- Championship outright winners (NFL Super Bowl, NBA Champion, F1 Drivers' Champion, Wimbledon, UEFA Champions League, etc.)
- Long-running reality TV competition winners (Survivor, Strictly Come Dancing)
- Season-long award predictions (Ballon d'Or, Heisman, MVP awards)
- Horse racing Triple Crown picks (each race individually)

**Season 1 pre-season pick examples:**
- Super Bowl LXI champion
- NBA 2026-27 champion
- F1 2027 Drivers' Champion
- UEFA Champions League 2026-27 winner
- Wimbledon 2027 Men's / Women's Singles winner
- 2026 Ballon d'Or winner
- Nobel Peace Prize 2026 laureate
- Eurovision 2027 winner
- Tour de France 2027 GC winner
- Giro d'Italia 2027 GC winner
- PDC World Darts Championship 2026-27 winner
- World Snooker Championship 2027 winner
- IPL 2027 champion

---

## Monthly Picks (M)

Monthly picks open on the 1st and close on the 7th of each month. They target events in that calendar month where recent-form context (current-season stats, draw, injuries) meaningfully informs the pick.

**Good candidates:**
- Grand Slam tennis singles champions (Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon, US Open)
- Major golf tournament winners (Masters, US Open, PGA Championship, The Open)
- Monthly F1 race winners for marquee rounds
- Sumo basho yusho winners
- WMM marathon winners (Tokyo, Boston, London, Berlin, Chicago, NYC)

**Season 1 monthly pick examples:**
- Australian Open 2027 men's / women's singles
- 2027 Masters Tournament winner
- 2027 French Open men's / women's singles
- Hatsu / Haru / Natsu / Nagoya / Aki / Kyushu Basho yusho winners
- Royal Ascot 2027 Gold Cup winner
- 24 Hours of Le Mans 2027 class winner
- Kentucky Derby 2027 winner
- IPL 2027 Orange Cap / Purple Cap

---

## Pop Props (X)

Pop props have the shortest window and the highest engagement. They are announced in-app when the window opens — players get a push notification and typically 24–72 hours to submit.

**Good candidates:**
- Award show winner predictions (Oscars Best Picture, Grammy AOTY, Emmy Drama Series)
- Reality TV finale picks (Survivor, Love Island, DWTS Mirrorball, The Traitors winner)
- One-night sporting spectacles (Super Bowl winner + halftime act, NBA Christmas Day upsets)
- Cultural moments (Met Gala "best-dressed," UK Christmas #1, Spotify Wrapped top artist)
- Niche champs (Cooper's Hill Cheese Rolling, Nathan's Hot Dog Eating, Wife Carrying)

**Season 1 pop prop examples:**
- 99th Oscars Best Picture
- 69th Grammy Awards AOTY
- Super Bowl LXI winner + halftime performer
- Emmy Outstanding Drama Series
- Love Island UK 2027 winner
- The Traitors UK S5 winner
- DWTS S35 Mirrorball winner
- UK Christmas Number One (2026)
- Time Person of the Year 2026
- 2027 NFL Draft Round 1 Pick #1
- LoL Worlds 2026 champion
- Valorant Champions 2026 winner

---

## Bonus Window Events

Some events are tagged as **bonus window** events — they cluster with other big events in the same month and earn 1.5× points when correct. Bonus window status is shown on the event card in-app.

---

## Verification at a Glance

| Type | Who verifies | Turnaround |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **A** — Automated | Official API / data feed | ≤ 24 hrs after result |
| **M** — Manual | Commissioner | ≤ 48 hrs after result |

Manual verification events (38% of catalogue) include: Met Gala best-dressed, Nobel Literature, Booker Prize, BBC Sports Personality, Person of the Year, sumo basho, niche championships, and most reality TV finales.
